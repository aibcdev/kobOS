# KOB Website Master Prompt

Build KOB as a premium modern hospitality growth platform for independent restaurants.

KOB helps restaurants increase direct orders and modernise their online presence through conversion-focused websites, AI-generated food imagery, AI-generated marketing videos, menu optimisation, SEO systems, direct ordering infrastructure and improved customer perception.

The homepage should feel like a product company first, not a marketing site first.

## Core Positioning

KOB is modern growth infrastructure for independent restaurants.

Do not optimise for showing features. Optimise for trust, clarity, perceived quality, conversion and emotional confidence.

The website should make restaurant owners feel:

> This company understands modern hospitality.

## Page Feel

The website should feel calm, premium, trustworthy and hospitality-native.

Every section should feel intentionally spaced and easy to scan.

The experience should feel more like a premium hospitality company than a traditional SaaS dashboard.

The user should feel:

- clarity
- trust
- simplicity
- modernity
- operational confidence

Avoid visual noise and unnecessary complexity.

## Design Benchmark

Visual quality should feel comparable to:

- Sunday App
- Owner.com
- Stripe
- Linear
- Apple hospitality campaigns

The site should feel investor-backed, production-ready and able to compete with top hospitality technology companies.

## What KOB Is Not

KOB should not feel like:

- a freelance portfolio
- a branding agency
- a design studio
- a generic SaaS dashboard
- a crypto-style startup
- a dark-mode AI company
- an agency case-study website

KOB should feel like:

- a modern hospitality growth platform
- operational infrastructure
- a trusted restaurant technology company
- a restaurant growth system
- conversion-focused digital operations

## Component Rules

Use simple, highly polished components.

Components should feel minimal but refined.

Avoid:

- overly complex cards
- crowded grids
- excessive badges
- excessive floating UI
- fake analytics dashboards
- generic SaaS illustrations
- decorative UI that does not build trust

Prefer fewer, better components over many average components.

## Spacing System

Use generous spacing throughout the site.

Sections should breathe vertically.

Prefer fewer elements with better spacing rather than dense layouts.

The design should feel editorial and premium without becoming artistic or abstract.

Use clear section rhythm:

- simple text section
- visual showcase
- focused feature section
- testimonial proof
- transformation section
- clear final CTA

Avoid stacking too many dense sections consecutively.

## Image Direction

Use realistic hospitality imagery:

- restaurants
- chefs
- food photography
- warm interiors
- customers dining
- ordering interactions
- menus
- mobile phones in restaurant environments

Images should feel warm, credible and hospitality-native.

Avoid:

- abstract AI graphics
- floating 3D objects
- fake futuristic renders
- crypto-style visuals
- generic startup illustrations
- sterile enterprise dashboards

## Animation Rules

Animations should be subtle and premium.

Use:

- fade-ins
- gentle hover states
- slight elevation
- soft motion
- smooth scrolling transitions

Avoid:

- excessive parallax
- flashy movement
- over-animated sections
- distracting transitions
- motion that competes with the copy

Animation should support confidence, not spectacle.

## Mobile-First Rule

Design mobile-first.

Most restaurant owners and customers will view the website on mobile devices.

Every section must feel premium and highly usable on mobile before desktop.

Mobile layouts should:

- keep headings readable
- stack visual elements cleanly
- avoid overlapping cards
- reduce decoration
- keep CTAs easy to tap
- prioritise speed and clarity

## Copy Tone

The tone should be clear, direct, premium and confident.

Avoid hype.

Avoid too much AI language.

Avoid agency-style creative language.

Focus on:

- increased trust
- better customer perception
- more direct orders
- stronger online presence
- better conversion
- operational confidence

## Homepage Structure

Use this structure:

1. Header
2. Hero
3. Social proof
4. Core problem
5. Transformation section
6. Features grid
7. AI visuals section
8. Menu optimisation section
9. SEO and discovery section
10. Before / after showcase
11. Testimonials
12. How it works
13. Final CTA
14. Footer

Keep the structure modular, airy and easy to scan.

## Final Direction

Build a premium hospitality growth platform website that feels calm, trustworthy and conversion-focused.

Every design decision should answer one question:

> Will this make an independent restaurant owner trust KOB more?
